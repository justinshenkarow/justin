What happens when your mom becomes your momager?

Justin Shenkarow had it all at age 12 — series regular roles, a face on lunchboxes, the whole child star package. Then puberty happened.

Now he's 45, still grinding in the voice-over booth, and his biggest fan (his mother) has decided it's time for a comeback. Her plan? Sell the house, move in, and document the whole disaster for reality TV.

The only problem? Justin's animated characters have opinions. Loud ones.

"It's Your Fault" is a live-action/animation hybrid comedy where the voices in Justin's head are literally the characters he's played for 30 years — and they're not shy about telling him what to do.

Why I Made This

I've been a working voice actor for 30 years. I've played characters that shaped childhoods. And I've watched every pilot I've pitched get notes like "love it, but who's the audience?"

So I made a show where I AM the audience.

"It's Your Fault" is about what happens when your dreams don't die, they just... mutate. It's about family, failure, and the voices in your head that won't shut up. It's also really, really funny.

My actual mother is in this. My animated characters are in this. Harold gives his mother notes. It's the most personal and most insane thing I've ever made.

Now I need your help getting someone to say yes.

— Justin
